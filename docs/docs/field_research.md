Observation Note.
Date: 26,Mar, 2026
Time: Afternoon
Location: Campus area and local market

Findings:
a)	Hawkers sell second-hand clothes on streets with no fixed locations
b)	Items displayed on ground or carried by hawkers walking around
c)	Poor lighting conditions at dusk/dawn make it hard to see item condition

Year: Year 4 Student  
Date: March 26, 2026

Key Responses:

	Yes, we do. It's challenging because we don't have any place to actually go.

User Interview
Interviewee: Kaneza Devine  
Year: Student  
Date: 27,Mar, 2026  
Duration: 3 minutes

Key Responses:

a)	Have you ever bought or sold second-hand clothing?
	I bought second-hand clothes before. Mostly from Instagram pages and local thrift markets. The experience was mixed. Sometimes I found really good quality items, but other times what I received didn't look exactly like the picture.
b)	What problems do you face?
	The main problem is trust. It's hard to know if the clothes are truly in good condition or if the seller is being honest. Another issue is communication. Sometimes sellers take too long to respond or don't give enough details. Also, delivery can be slow or unreliable.
c)	How do you currently find second-hand clothes?
	I usually find them on Instagram thrift pages, WhatsApp groups or status updates. Sometimes local markets or thrift shops.
d)	What would make it easier?
	A platform or an app would help a lot. Somewhere everything is organized in one place. It would be easier if there are clear pictures and descriptions, prices are fixed and transparent, there is a simple way to contact sellers, and delivery is reliable.
e)	Have you had a bad experience?
	I once bought a dress online that looked very nice in the picture. But when it arrived, the quality was poor and it had damages that were not shown. It was disappointing and I couldn't return it.
f)	Issue with sizing?
	Yes, this happens a lot. Sizes are not always accurate, especially with second-hand products. Sometimes they say one size, but when you try it on, it doesn't fit. This makes online buying risky since you can't try before buying.

Core Problem Statement:
Students at campus face significant challenges when buying and selling second-hand fashion items due to the lack of a centralized, reliable platform. Current methods such as street hawkers, Instagram thrift pages, and WhatsApp groups lead to trust issues, difficulty finding specific items, inability to verify size and fit before purchase, poor product visibility, misleading images, unreliable delivery, and no quality assessment. These friction points result in wasted money, mismatched items, and a frustrating experience that discourages students from engaging in second-hand fashion.

Real-World Constraints Mapping
CONSTRAINT	HOW IT RELATES TO OUR MAPPING
Limited technical infrastructure	Students currently rely on Instagram, WhatsApp, and street hawkers because there's no dedicated platform. Our app must work on various devices and within campus network limitations.
User trust and safety concerns	Our findings show trust issues, misleading images, inaccurate sizing, and no return policy. The app must address trust through seller ratings, detailed product descriptions, size guides, clear photos, and user reviews.
Resource constraints (time, budget)	As students building this app, we must focus on MVP features that solve the core problems (search/filter, detailed listings with size/condition info, user ratings, in-app chat) within the course timeline.
